Lent

A season of penance and preparation

What is Lent?

Lent is the 40-day liturgical season of preparation for Easter, beginning on Ash Wednesday and ending on Holy Saturday. It is a period of fasting, prayer, and repentance observed by Catholics and many other Christians worldwide.

The 40 days recall the 40 days Jesus spent fasting in the wilderness (Matthew 4:1-11) before beginning His public ministry. Lent is a time to turn away from sin and be faithful to the Gospel.

The Three Pillars of Lent

🙏

Prayer

Deepening our relationship with God through daily prayer, Scripture reading, and reflection.

🥕

Fasting

Making sacrifices as a form of spiritual discipline, offering up our sufferings to God.

❤️

Charity

Giving to those in need through almsgiving and acts of mercy.

Ash Wednesday

Lent begins with Ash Wednesday, when Catholics receive ashes on their foreheads as a sign of repentance.
"Remember that you are dust, and to dust you shall return." (Genesis 3:19)

Fasting and Abstinence: Catholics aged 18-59 are obliged to fast on Ash Wednesday and Good Friday (one full meal, two smaller meals). All Catholics aged 14 and above abstain from meat on Ash Wednesday and all Fridays of Lent.

Holy Week

  • Palm Sunday: Commemorates Jesus' triumphant entry into Jerusalem
  • Maundy Thursday: Remembers the Last Supper and the institution of the Eucharist
  • Good Friday: Commemorates the crucifixion and death of Jesus
  • Holy Saturday: A day of waiting and reflection before the Easter Vigil
  • Easter Sunday: The glorious resurrection of our Lord Jesus Christ!

Lenten Prayers

Prayer of Surrender

Oh Jesus, I surrender myself to You. Take care of everything. I offer You this Lenten season as a time of renewal and conversion. Help me to die to sin and live for You alone. Amen.

Lenten Prayer

Lord Jesus, during this holy season of Lent, grant me the grace to turn away from sin and be faithful to the Gospel. Help me to pray more deeply, fast with joy, and give generously to those in need. Through Your passion and cross, bring me to the glory of Your resurrection. Amen.
