Way Of The Cross

The Stations of the Cross

The Stations of the Cross

A traditional devotion meditating on the passion and death of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Preparatory Prayer

My Lord Jesus Christ, You made this journey to die for me with love unutterable. I have so many times ungratefully abandoned You. But now I love You with all my heart. I am sorry for my sins. Grant that I may love You always. I will never abandon You again.

Station I: Jesus is Condemned to Death

"Pilate said to them, 'Then what shall I do with Jesus who is called Christ?' They all said, 'Let him be crucified!'" - Matthew 27:22-26

Lord, give us the strength to accept life's judgments with humility and to stand with You when the world condemns truth.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station II: Jesus Carries His Cross

"They pressed into service a passer-by, Simon of Cyrene, to carry His cross." - Mark 15:21

Lord, help us to embrace our crosses daily and follow You with courage and love.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station III: Jesus Falls the First Time

"He was wounded for our transgressions, crushed for our iniquities." - Isaiah 53:5

Lord, when we fall into sin, grant us the grace to rise again and continue our journey toward You.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station IV: Jesus Meets His Mother

"Simeon said to Mary, 'A sword will pierce your own soul too.'" - Luke 2:34-35

Mary, Mother of Sorrows, pray for us and comfort us in our times of suffering.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station V: Simon Helps Jesus Carry the Cross

"They compelled a man named Simon of Cyrene to carry His cross." - Matthew 27:32

Lord, open our eyes to see those who are burdened and give us the strength to help them carry their crosses.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station VI: Veronica Wipes the Face of Jesus

"I was hungry and you gave me food, I was thirsty and you gave me drink." - Matthew 25:35

Lord, give us the courage to show kindness in the face of suffering and to see Your face in those who suffer.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station VII: Jesus Falls the Second Time

"Surely He has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows." - Isaiah 53:4

Lord, lift us up when we fall and renew our strength to continue following You.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station VIII: Jesus Meets the Women of Jerusalem

"Daughters of Jerusalem, do not weep for Me, but weep for yourselves and for your children." - Luke 23:28

Lord, touch our hearts with true sorrow for our sins and grant us the grace of conversion.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station IX: Jesus Falls the Third Time

"He was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows and acquainted with grief." - Isaiah 53:3

Lord, in our weakness be our strength. When we can go no further, carry us in Your loving arms.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station X: Jesus is Stripped of His Garments

"They divided My garments among them, and for My clothing they cast lots." - Psalm 22:18

Lord, strip away our pride and selfishness, and clothe us with humility and love.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station XI: Jesus is Nailed to the Cross

"When they came to the place called the Skull, they crucified Him." - Luke 23:33

Lord, forgive us and all who sin. Nail our sins to Your cross that we may be free.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station XII: Jesus Dies on the Cross

"Jesus cried out with a loud voice, 'Father, into Your hands I commend My spirit.' And He breathed His last." - Luke 23:46

Lord, into Your hands we commend our spirit. Grant us the grace to die to sin and live for You.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station XIII: Jesus is Taken Down from the Cross

"Joseph of Arimathea took the body of Jesus and wrapped it in a linen cloth." - Matthew 27:59-60

Lord, let us hold You close in our hearts. May Your mother, who held You in her arms, intercede for us.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Station XIV: Jesus is Laid in the Tomb

"They laid Jesus in a new tomb hewn out of rock." - Mark 15:46

Lord, grant us rest in You and fill us with the hope of the resurrection. May we rise with You to new life.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

Concluding Prayer

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You, because by Your holy cross You have redeemed the world. We thank You, Lord, for the gift of Your passion and death. Grant that we may carry our crosses with patience and love, and one day share in Your glorious resurrection. Amen.
