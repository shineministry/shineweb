Pope Leo XIV

Robert Francis Prevost

267th Pontiff of the Catholic Church

Elected: May 8, 2025

Early Life

Robert Francis Prevost was born on September 14, 1955, in Chicago, Illinois, USA. He is the first American to be elected Pope in the history of the Catholic Church.

Religious Life

He entered the Order of Saint Augustine (Augustinians) and professed his vows in 1977. He was ordained a priest in 1982. Fluent in five languages (English, Spanish, Italian, French, and Portuguese), he was sent as a missionary to Peru where he served from 1985 to 1998.

Leadership Roles

  • 2001-2013: Prior General of the Order of Saint Augustine (worldwide head of the Augustinian Order)
  • 2014: Appointed Bishop of Chiclayo, Peru
  • 2023: Appointed Prefect of the Dicastery for Bishops (Vatican office overseeing bishop appointments worldwide)
  • 2025: Elected Pope, taking the name Leo XIV in honor of Pope Leo XIII

Papal Vision

Pope Leo XIV's pontificate is characterized by a focus on social justice, inclusivity, environmental stewardship, and global outreach. His experience as a missionary in Latin America shapes his pastoral approach to the universal Church.

Connection to India

Pope Leo XIV visited India twice (2004 and 2006), traveling to Kerala and Tamil Nadu. These visits gave him firsthand experience of the vibrant Catholic faith in India.

Prayer for the Pope

Heavenly Father, we thank You for giving us Pope Leo XIV as our Holy Father. Grant him wisdom, strength, and courage to lead Your Church faithfully. Protect him and guide him by the power of Your Holy Spirit. May he be a true shepherd after Your own heart, leading all people to the saving knowledge of Your Son, Jesus Christ. Through Christ our Lord. Amen.
