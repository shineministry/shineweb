Our Lady Of Fatima Shrine, Karjat

A blessed place of pilgrimage and prayer

"Fatima of the East"

History of the Shrine

The story of Our Lady of Fatima Shrine in Karjat begins on March 5, 1915, when the Catholic mission first arrived in the region. The devotion to Our Lady of Fatima in Karjat started among railway workers who worked on the Mumbai-Pune railway line.

In 1929, Fr. Orphine DeSa began serving the Catholic community in Karjat. The devotion grew steadily through the 1930s, with significant milestones in 1936, 1937, and 1939.

A key moment came when a statue of Our Lady of Fatima was brought from Fatima, Portugal, to Karjat. This statue became the focal point of devotion at the shrine.

Construction and Expansion

The land for the shrine was purchased and a chapel was built under the leadership of Fr. Stephen Pereira. The shrine faced several challenges over the years, including tax issues, lack of priests, and discontinued train services that affected pilgrim access.

Connection to Fatima, Portugal

The shrine's spirituality is deeply rooted in the Fatima apparitions of 1917, when the Blessed Virgin Mary appeared to three shepherd children - Lucia, Francisco, and Jacinta - in Fatima, Portugal. Our Lady delivered the Three Secrets of Fatima and the Miracle of the Sun was witnessed by thousands.

Francisco and Jacinta Marto were canonized as saints on May 13, 2017, by Pope Francis.

Present Day

Today, the shrine is served by the MSFS (Missionaries of St. Francis de Sales) and the Jesuits. There are plans for the shrine's elevation to Archdiocesan Shrine status under the Archdiocese of Bombay.

Mass Timings

  • Weekdays: 6:30 AM
  • Sundays: 7:00 AM, 9:00 AM
  • First Saturday: Special Fatima Mass

Feast Day

May 13th - The feast of Our Lady of Fatima, celebrated with special Masses, processions, and devotions.
